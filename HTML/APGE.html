INDIAN JUNGLE (ARTICLE)

India is home to a diverse range of jungles that showcase the country's rich biodiversity and natural beauty. One such notable jungle is the Sundarbans, located in the eastern part of India, primarily in the state of West Bengal and extending into neighboring Bangladesh. The Sundarbans is the largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is renowned for its unique ecosystem, where the land and water intertwine. The jungle gets its name from the Sundari trees, which dominate the region and provide a vital habitat for numerous species.
